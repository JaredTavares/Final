Police Training

Overview

The Recruit Training instructors prepare those seeking a career in law enforcement with the fundamentals of police service. The Professional Training instructors provide continuing education opportunities to further skills and career abilities. The Leadership Academy provides a path for lifelong learners to enhance their skills as core leaders, not only in the law enforcement arena, but in all areas of life. The Drivers Training staff hones the skills of public servants in the areas of low speed and emergency vehicle operation. The Firearms instructors provide recruit training, as well as continuing education training.

Physical Training

  • The 75-Yard Pursuit Run
  • 1.5 Mile Run
  • Push Ups
  • Sit Ups

How much to complete

The 75-Yard Pursuit Run prepares you to be fast, agile, and ready to do an all out sprint, which is exactly what this test evaluates. Doing push-ups prepare you for wrestling perpetrators down to the ground once you finally catch them after your foot pursuit(You have two minutes to complete as many pushups as possible, and only proper ones are counted). Sit-ups test is not assessing long-term back health on the job, it does adequately assess your baseline core endurance(You have two minutes to complete as many sit ups as possible, and only proper ones are counted). The 1.5 Mile run will be rare that you run a mile and a half during your shift, this test will serve to assess your aerobic capacity.

Training techniques


Preparing: Usually, police departments use one of two types of tests: a physical abilities test or a physical fitness assessment. The best way to prepare for both is to do exercises that build up both your physical strength and your cardiovascular abilities. Regardless, consult with your doctor before you begin any fitness regimen, and start working toward getting in shape well before you apply for the job so you'll be ready whenever it comes time for the physical assessment.
